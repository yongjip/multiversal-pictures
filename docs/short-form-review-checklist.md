# Short-Form Review Checklist

Use this checklist before approving a final Shorts master.

## Packaging

- The title, hook, and thumbnail all push the same claim.
- The video makes sense even if the speaker's name is removed.
- The first proof point arrives by beat 2 or 3.
- The script stays within 25 to 35 seconds.
- The script uses 5 to 7 lines and one idea per line.

## Narration

- The opening line is understandable within 1 to 2 seconds.
- The voice sounds calm, intelligent, and controlled.
- No line sounds like ad copy or trailer narration.
- No sentence feels too long for one breath.
- Unintended gaps between spoken lines stay under 0.6 seconds.
- Silent tails stay under 0.5 seconds except for the final payoff beat.
- `cedar` and `marin` were compared before the final choice.

## Visuals

- Each shot has one clear read on mobile.
- Each shot shows a clear motion change, proof change, or compositional reveal.
- No generated readable code, UI, logs, charts, or labels appear.
- Human shots stay medium or wide unless there is a strong reason not to.
- There are no anatomy, motion, or continuity failures.
- The lower subtitle-safe area stays clean.

## Hybrid Proof

- Each proof beat is explicitly tagged as AI plate, overlay plate, or manual proof.
- Overlay plates leave enough clean space for the final real proof insert.
- Manual proof beats use exact UI or screen captures only where precision matters.
- The hybrid inserts do not introduce a second unrelated claim.

## Final Master

- The final pass used `storybook-pro-vertical`.
- The final pass used `master`.
- Anchors were used for human/workspace shots.
- Review ran in `repair` mode with `best_of >= 2`.
- Selected review scores are all `>= 0.84`.
- Burned subtitles remain readable over every shot.

## Publish Gate

- The private upload succeeded.
- The Studio preview looks correct on mobile.
- The description and title match the approved package.
- The final video is safe to publish without additional factual edits.
